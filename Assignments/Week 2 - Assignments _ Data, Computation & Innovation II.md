### **Deliverables**

**Step 1 — Form a team and pick a topic**

Get into groups of 3-5. As a team, pick one of the following broad topics. It should be something with enough data for each person to create a chart from.

---

**Step 2 — Set up your team repo**

One person on the team should clone the weekly template and create a new GitHub repository for your "chart blog."

Add your teammates as collaborators under Settings \> Collaborators on GitHub. Everyone should clone the repo to their own machine.

To prevent conflicts, I would encourage you to work in branches. Here are guides to branching with [git](https://git-scm.com/book/en/v2/Git-Branching-Branches-in-a-Nutshell) and [GitHub Desktop](https://docs.github.com/en/desktop/making-changes-in-a-branch/managing-branches-in-github-desktop).

---

**Step 4 — Each person builds a chart**

Everyone on the team independently builds one chart related to your topic. 

To add your chart to the blog:

1. Make a copy of \_StoryCardTemplate.svelte and put it into the stories folder  
2. Rename it something descriptive to either you or the content — JaredChart.svelte, TemperatureTrend.svelte, etc.  
3. Build your chart inside it  
4. Import and add it to Blog.svelte alongside your teammates' charts

Coordinate with your team so you're not all building the same chart.

---

**Step 4 — Submit**

One person on the team emails me the link to your GitHub repository before next class. Everyone submits a screenshot of their chart and a brief reflection via Courseworks.

---

**Requirements**

* One chart per person, minimum  
* Each chart uses at least one D3 scale  
* Charts are on a shared topic but do not need to use the same dataset  
* A short title and 1-2 sentence of chatter per chart, written inside the story card
